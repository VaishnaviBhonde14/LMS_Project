üë§ Who is a Business Analyst?

A Business Analyst is a professional who analyzes business processes, identifies areas for improvement, gathers and documents requirements, and ensures effective communication between stakeholders and technical teams.

In simple terms, BAs help organizations solve business problems using data, processes, and technology.

üìå Key Responsibilities of a Business Analyst

While the exact responsibilities vary by domain (IT, finance, healthcare, etc.), most Business Analysts typically perform the following:

  • Requirement Gathering: Conduct interviews, workshops, and research to understand stakeholder needs.
  • Process Analysis: Map current workflows and suggest improvements using tools like flowcharts or BPMN.
  • Documentation: Create BRDs, FRDs, and user stories.
  • Stakeholder Communication: Act as a liaison between clients, developers, testers, and management.
  • Solution Design Support: Work with development teams to ensure solutions meet business goals.
  • Testing & Validation: Assist in UAT and validate delivered features.
  • Data Analysis: Use tools to analyze KPIs and identify inefficiencies.

üéØ Core Skills Required for a Business Analyst

To thrive as a Business Analyst, one must blend analytical thinking, communication skills, and domain knowledge.

1. Analytical and Problem-Solving Skills
  • Break down complex problems
  • Identify patterns and root causes
  • Recommend logical and feasible solutions
2. Communication & Interpersonal Skills
  • Conduct effective stakeholder interviews
  • Create clear and concise documentation
  • Bridge communication gaps between tech and non-tech teams
3. Documentation & Modeling Tools
  • BRD, FRD, Use Case, User Stories
  • Process modeling using BPMN, UML
  • Wireframing (Balsamiq, Figma)
4. Domain Knowledge
  • Understand business domain (Banking, Retail, Healthcare, etc.)
  • Be aware of industry-specific challenges and terminology
5. Technical Awareness
  • Understand APIs, databases, software systems
  • Knowledge of SDLC models
  • Basic SQL, Excel, and BI tools (Power BI, Tableau)
6. Tools Proficiency

Some common tools every BA should know:

Jira, Confluence, Excel, SQL, Trello, Asana, MS Word, Google Docs, Draw.io, Lucidchart, Power BI, MySQL, Teams, Zoom
